Discussion Title: Is modern psychology is a pseudoscience?

1. Modern psychology is a pseudoscience
1.1. Pro: Methodologies used in psychological and behavioral studies have poor [statistical power](http://coneixement.info/blog/?p=267).
1.2. Pro: An overwhelming majority of studies conducted in the field [cannot be replicated successfully](https://www.vox.com/science-and-health/2018/8/27/17761466/psychology-replication-crisis-nature-social-science).
1.3. Pro: Many of its theories are created and used to push an agenda in a social context.
1.3.1. Pro: [Affluenza](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2018/apr/03/affluenza-teen-ethan-couch-leaves-texas-jail-two-years)- a recognized mental disorder, is an example of a creation of a theory to explain away the bad behavior of the socioeconomically affluent.
1.3.2. Con: Mental illness does not abdicate the consequences or accountability of someone's actions
1.4. Con: Psychology is recognized as a valid science in most academic institutions.
1.5. Con: There are deterministic laws of human behavior and psychology is a scientific attempt to understand them.
1.5.1. Con: These attempts are made using [scientificly dubious](https://arachnoid.com/trouble_with_psychology/?fbclid=IwAR0HWIq8s1BWafFAzE5ekhW3Jte9OfOJASu61nx2eAN1k-Xve6uPzeh7xcs) methods.
1.6. Pro: Many of its theories are open to interpretation.
1.6.1. Con: Many of the theories in science are open to interpretation. including medical science. All of these fields though have documented facts which have been established through rigorous, proper application of the scientific method. That is what makes them sciences, and not "pseudosciences".
1.6.1.1. Con: Unlike medical and other sciences, most theories in psychology are [unfalsifiable](https://medium.com/\@richarddmorey/new-paper-why-most-of-psychology-is-statistically-unfalsifiable-4c3b6126365a) through lack of experimental rigor, and this puts it into question the validity of the field as a "science".
