Discussion Title: Should the UN move to protect the Rohingyas?

1. The UN should move to protect the Rohingyas.
1.1. Con: There is no legal base for the UN to act in regard to the Rohingya question.
1.1.1. Pro: The Rohingya problem is not threatening international peace and security as specified in [Art 1 of the UN Charter](http://www.un.org/en/sections/un-charter/chapter-i/index.html).
1.1.1.1. Con: Myanmar's government has violated the[Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide.](http://www.un.org/en/genocideprevention/documents/atrocity-crimes/Doc.1_Convention%20on%20the%20Prevention%20and%20Punishment%20of%20the%20Crime%20of%20Genocide.pdf)Therefore, there are grounds for a UN intervention in accordance with Article 1.
1.1.2. Con: The UN has a legal [responsibility to protect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Responsibility_to_protect).
1.2. Con: China is supportive of Myanmar. Any intervention will lack their support, and may turn China against the UN.
1.3. Pro: Genocide should always be opposed.
1.3.1. Con: The UN does not need to be the opposition, it serves a better purpose as a unifying body and space for discussion and cooperation.
1.3.1.1. Con: Outside of the UN mandate, any intervention will be illegal. The UN is the only suitable actor in this scenario.
1.4. Pro: The attacks on the Rohingyas serve to radicalize them, feeding a cycle of retaliatory strikes that can only be stopped by a third party.
1.5. Con: The UN's history of interventionism is weak.
1.5.1. Con: This is only because of the Council of Security where the permanent members have a right to veto, and use it to protect their national interests.
1.6. Con: The UN was wrong to label the situation as genocide.
1.6.1. Con: The Myanmar government has not allowed human rights investigators into the country, so they must have something to hide.
1.7. Pro: A successful intervention could radically improve the UN's image.
