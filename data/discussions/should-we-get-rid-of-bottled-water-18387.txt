Discussion Title: Should We Get Rid of Bottled Water?

1. We should get rid of bottled water.
1.1. Pro: We need to address this issue, but we need a wider variety of options to allow consumers to get lower cost water.
1.2. Con: Homeless people will lose a source of extra income when they cannot collect a deposit on used water bottles.
1.2.1. Pro: In [Germany](https://www.dw.com/en/empty-bottles-help-germans-make-ends-meet/a-15408472), many in poverty collect bottles to make some extra income.
1.2.2. Con: Homeless people should be helped in other and more structural ways.
1.3. Pro: The best alternative to bottle water is refillable water bottles \(Nalgene etc.\). People need to be taught that refillable water bottles are far better and more eco-friendly. There are so many water bottles in the earth that they can wrap around the earth 190 times. [prezi.com](https://prezi.com/q6mzifjzvauq/how-many-water-bottles-can-go-around-the-earth-190-times/)
1.4. Pro: There are more than [50 billion plastic bottles](https://www.banthebottle.net/bottled-water-facts/) used each year; getting rid of the them will have a huge, fast, positive impact in the environment
1.5. Pro: It is not a sustainable solution.
1.6. Pro: Currently, bottled water contributes to the growing problem of plastic waste.
1.6.1. Pro: 22,000 tons of plastic ends up in the water every day: [Bottled water vs tap water](https://www.waterbenefitshealth.com/bottled-water-or-tap-water.html).
1.7. Con: There is a need for clean drinking water where none is available through tap or wells.
1.7.1. Pro: The wider deployment of water stations via locally filtered dispensing or in more route locations supply via bulk container fed stations is a viable affordable option
1.7.2. Con: Bottled water is [not as clean](https://abcnews.go.com/Business/study-bottled-water-safer-tap-water/story?id=87558) as one would think.
1.7.2.1. Pro: Bottled water is therefore not a necessary measure, and should be got rid of.
1.7.2.2. Con: It is, however, cleaner than other sources in most situations.
1.8. Con: Consumers should decide, not regulation.
1.9. Con: Personal liberty and choice is more important.
1.9.1. Con: Not at the cost of the planet.
