Discussion Title: Should The Media Be Forced To Report More Positive News?

1. The media should be forced to report more positive news.
1.1. Con: A free and open media should be supported.
1.1.1. Con: Freedom of speech in the media leads to biased and inaccurate reporting.
1.1.2. Pro: It is not the government's place to decide what is "good news" and what isn't.
1.1.3. Pro: Censoring the way that the news wishes to tell their stories could result in the censoring of other forms of media.
1.2. Con: Dropping negativity could lead to the failure of the journalism industry, and many people could lose jobs.
1.2.1. Pro: People are more likely to read and be interested in negative news stories because of our [negativity bias](https://bigthink.com/in-their-own-words/why-we-love-bad-news-understanding-negativity-bias).
1.2.2. Pro: [Employment](https://www.statista.com/statistics/315563/number-full-time-journalists-usa/) in the journalism industry is already dropping.
1.3. Pro: Media exaggerates the negative [for selfish reasons.](http://www.borgenmagazine.com/progress-in-the-world/)
1.3.1. Con: People will stop watching/reading if the media inaccurately portrays reality.
1.3.2. Pro: The media may exaggerate [negative stories](http://www.journalism.org/2017/10/02/covering-president-trump-in-a-polarized-media-environment/) about Donald Trump due to their ideological biases.
1.4. Pro: Exaggerating the negative can lead to [mental health issues](http://time.com/5125894/is-reading-news-bad-for-you/).
1.4.1. Pro: Exaggerating the negative can lead to hopelessness.
1.4.2. Con: This is an argument for why exaggerating negative news is bad, not for why media should be forced to do the opposite.
1.4.3. Con: If the news makes you mentally ill, then you just shouldn't watch it.
1.5. Pro: Focusing on more positive news will make people more optimistic about the world.
1.5.1. Pro: There are [many reasons](https://humanprogress.org/) to be optimistic.
1.5.2. Con: More positive coverage will make people complacent and unwilling to address the serious problems facing the world.
1.6. Con: Positive news stories are rarely as important as negative ones.
1.6.1. Con: Long-term trends like the reduction of [poverty](https://ourworldindata.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/World-Poverty-Since-1820.png), [hunger](https://www.vox.com/2014/10/13/6969953/malnutrition-undernutrition-getting-better), and [violence](https://slides.ourworldindata.org/war-and-violence/#/title-slide) are arguably much more important to humanity than short-term, negative news stories.
1.6.2. Pro: Negative stories should inspire change, whereas positive stories imply that things should stay as they are.
