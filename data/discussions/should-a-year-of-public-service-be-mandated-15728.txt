Discussion Title: Should a Year of Public Service be Mandated?

1. A Year of Public Service Should be Mandated.
1.1. Con: This would be a severe violation of personal freedom.
1.1.1. Pro: Nobody wishes to be forced to take a year of unpaid work when they could be in a normal job.
1.2. Con: Forcing people to volunteer is likely to cause antagonism, thus missing the desired purpose.
1.2.1. Con: Some countries already do this and if you have the right cultural attitude it is seen in a positive light.
1.2.1.1. Con: This forces a narrow view of what is the 'right' cultural attitude.
1.2.2. Pro: It could be practically problematic too, if somebody is struggling to support themselves.
1.2.3. Pro: -> See 1.1.1.
1.3. Pro: It would push people out of their comfort zone, and teach them the skills of flexibility and adaptation.
1.4. Pro: People should not always get paid to get jobs done, especially ones that are vital and cannot be afforded. People can gain skills in budgeting and learning how to give freely too.
1.5. Pro: It would promote patriotism from a younger age, connecting people to a common cause of freedom and justice.
1.5.1. Con: Patriotism is not a universally held belief nor aim, and so should not be enforced on those who do not ascribe any worth to it.
1.5.2. Con: There is a difference between inspiring unity and patriotism; the latter is specific and limited, whilst the former a broader and more desirable aim.
1.5.3. Pro: Even for those who are not patriotic, the values it engenders are vital.
1.5.4. Pro: Mandatory service [musters](http://verdemagazine.com/the-effects-of-compulsory-service-on-patriotism-2) a sense of patriotism, because all citizens internalise the idea that it is right to give to your country.
1.6. Pro: It would expand possible career opportunities.
1.6.1. Pro: Volunteer work [looks good on a CV](https://www.hercampus.com/career/job-advice/how-important-having-volunteer-work-your-resume).
1.6.1.1. Pro: It shows organisation, commitment and a sense of social responsibility.
1.6.1.1.1. Con: The latter is negated if it is forced onto people who do not want to do it.
1.6.1.2. Con: Other work looks equally good, and would earn the person in question money to live on.
1.6.2. Pro: Military service provides numerous transferable [skills](https://www.ctp.org.uk/focus/tips-and-advice-cv-tips-6-steps-to-translate/486752) such as leadership, problem solving and an excellent work ethic.
1.6.3. Con: Military service is a huge time commitment. This time could be better used to create other opportunities. For example, individuals could attend [university](https://www.theguardian.com/news/datablog/2010/jul/15/employment-statistics-university-graduates).
1.7. Pro: It would allow easier access to graduate school programs.
1.7.1. Con: If every one is taking a year of public service it won't be anything special and thus not affect the access to graduate programs.
1.7.2. Pro: Can be an asset in assisting students with higher education cost concerns.
1.8. Con: It could result in a culture shock.
1.8.1. Con: Culture shocks broaden the horizon.
1.8.2. Con: Public service would help culture individuals at a young age, thus promoting common understanding among racial and cultural lines.
1.8.3. Pro: For those who have come from sheltered backgrounds, with perhaps overprotective parents, the shock may be detrimental.
1.9. Con: Isolation from family could be a concern.
1.9.1. Pro: Young people depend on their families and friends; without this, they lose a vital support.
1.10. Con: Public service can be mentally, physically, and emotionally challenging.
1.10.1. Con: This could be an excellent learning curve for young people, who can go out into the world better prepared.
1.10.2. Pro: This could put a person's [safety at risk](https://www.nytimes.com/2011/05/11/us/11corps.html), as people go to unsafe places and get diseases in the Peace Corps.
1.10.3. Pro: Those with mental health problems may find it particularly difficult to cope.
1.10.4. Pro: Military service is causally [linked](https://www.verywellmind.com/overview-of-ptsd-and-the-military-2797443) to post traumatic stress disorder.
