Discussion Title: Was the decision to ban Alex Jones from Social Media sites the right decision?

1. Was it right to ban Alex Jones and Inforwars from Social Media sites such as Facebook and YouTube.
1.1. Con: Banning Alex Jones gave him media attention outside his audience.
1.1.1. Pro: It might have been possible to quietly and incrementally remove Alex Jones' content, although he might have noticed such actions and complained to his followers.
1.1.1.1. Pro: Making Alex Jones disappear would have been easier than hoping he wouldn't overreact to any attack on him by social media outlets.
1.1.2. Pro: Banning Alex Jones made him a martyr for a cause those who banned him did not agree with.
1.2. Pro: Alex Jones had irresponsibly spread false rumours such as the [Pizzagate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pizzagate_conspiracy_theory) conspiracy theory which led to violence.
1.3. Pro: Alex Jones has been proven to be incorrect on many statements, leading to injury or harassment towards people. [Sandy Hook Parents Forced To Move 7 Times Over Harassment](https://www.timesofisrael.com/parents-of-jewish-sandy-hook-victim-forced-to-move-7-times-due-to-harassment/)
1.4. Con: This sets a precedent in which platforms can use moral a obligation to shut down other platforms and speakers alike.
1.5. Con: Infowars and Alex Jones are using their 1st Amendment right to speak freely on opinions they hold.
1.5.1. Con: Alex Jones has exceeded the boundaries of free speech by defaming people, for instance claiming that Hillary Clinton has "personally murdered and chopped up and raped" children.[adl.org](https://www.adl.org/resources/backgrounders/alex-jones-five-things-to-know) Such a serious accusation must be supported by evidence, which Jones has failed to provide.
1.5.2. Con: It is not Alex Jones 1st Amendment right to speak freely on these privately owned platforms.
1.6. Pro: Social Media sites have the right to remove any content they deem go against their policies.
1.6.1. Pro: The sites he was removed from are publishing platforms. Publishers reserve the right to not publish material.
1.6.2. Con: Though they are well within their legal rights to ban him, as virtual monopolies, sites like Facebook and YouTube have an increased social responsibility to uphold free speech. There should at least be some kind of public process before a person is removed.
1.6.3. Pro: The media platforms that banned him are private/for profit not governmental so they can do as they please re: constitutional rights to free speech. The problem lies in his nonsensical proclamations being presented as fact and not opinion so I agree with the ban.
1.7. Con: Alex Jones and Infowars are considered part of the media spectrum, which should be free and open to report.
1.7.1. Con: Extremists media can be limited if it saveguards the democracy, just as political parties can be prohibited for the same reason.
1.7.1.1. Pro: In Germany, an [extreme left wing party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Germany) was banned and a [right wing party](https://www.businessinsider.com/germany-is-trying-to-ban-a-far-right-political-party-but-it-could-backfire-2016-8?IR=T) was considered to be banned.
1.7.1.2. Con: Alex Jones has not called for direct violence against anyone or groups of people.
1.7.1.2.1. Con: Indirectly, his speech has let to the [harrassment](https://www.timesofisrael.com/parents-of-jewish-sandy-hook-victim-forced-to-move-7-times-due-to-harassment/) of Sandy Hook families.
1.7.1.2.2. Con: Alex Jones has threatened to attack Robert Muller in one of his videos on YouTube, saying that Muller is a "monster" and "demon" which he will "take down" or "die trying." [thehill.com](http://thehill.com/homenews/media/398512-alex-jones-threatens-mueller-youre-going-to-get-it-or-im-going-to-die-trying)
1.7.2. Con: -> See 1.6.1.
1.7.3. Con: Jones is entertainment. Media has sources to backup their narratives. Many of Jones' rants are baseless and inflammatory.
1.8. Pro: Infowars has broke several Terms of Service agreements, given temporary bans, and continued to break them, therefore the companies were acting the their and the public's best interest.
1.8.1. Con: It is not in the public's best interest to have restricted and censored speech on the most popular public discussion forums.
1.8.2. Con: The terms of service are arbitrarily enforced.
