Discussion Title: Oprah 2020: Should Oprah Run For President?

1. Oprah should run for president of the United States in 2020.
1.1. Con: Oprah promotes various ["snake oil" products](http://thefederalist.com/2018/01/09/remember-oprah-winfrey-premier-national-snake-oil-salesman/), which calls her integrity into question when it comes to running for office.
1.2. Pro: Oprah comes from humble origins, and has become successful despite past hardships. This is a quality greatly admired by many people.
1.2.1. Con: Humble origins and success in the face of adversity are indeed admirable, but other qualities are arguably more important for a potential presidential candidate.
1.2.1.1. Pro: Oprah has no substantive political experience.
1.2.1.1.1. Con: Donald Trump also had no political experience, now he is the president.
1.2.1.1.1.1. Con: The fact that he is president does not mean that he should be president.
1.2.1.1.1.2. Con: Donald Trump's lack of experience has arguably already led to many bad decisions for the US as a country.
1.2.1.1.2. Pro: Oprah has no foreign policy experience.
1.2.1.1.3. Pro: Donald Trump's lack of previous political experience was a significant argument to label him as unqualified.  Those who opposed Trump as a candidate at least in part for this reason can't logically conclude that Oprah makes a qualified candidate.
1.2.1.1.4. Pro: This level of politics is highly complicated and experience in lawmaking and foreign policy helps in decision making.
1.2.1.2. Pro: Experience holding public office \(at the state or local level\) or the military should be a prerequisite for the presidency. Since Oprah has neither, she should not run for president of the United States.
1.2.1.2.1. Pro: The President, especially, needs a record of being elected by the public, found worthy of being re-elected by the public, and have a record of being accountable to the public. The Presidency is not an entry-level position. If Oprah wants to get into politics, let her run for state legislature or, at the highest, a US House position first.
1.2.1.2.2. Pro: Most people who support Oprah's politics have argued that Trump is unqualified because he lacks public service experience. It would be hippocritical for these people to vote for Oprah.
1.2.1.2.3. Con: Holding public office and/or military experience should not be prerequisites for the presidency.
1.2.1.3. Pro: In her career, Oprah has not had to think deeply about issues pertaining to public policy or national security, both of which should be crucial for a presidential candidate.
1.3. Con: At the very least, a candidate should be interested in running for \(and winning\) the presidency. Oprah has recently stated that [she is not interested](https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/the-fix/wp/2018/01/25/oprah-says-shes-not-interested-but-americans-say-she-could-beat-trump/?utm_term=.ba991a444ab8) in running. So, she should not run.
1.4. Con: The American government is already criticized for the role corporations play in politics and for how wealthy many politicians are. An Oprah candidacy/presidency could set a dangerous precedent forcing the poor or middle class to accept that public discourse should be left to the top .01%
1.5. Con: The nomination of candidates like Oprah or Mark Zuckerberg, candidates with little political experience, would hamstring attempts by the democrats to have a coherent policy agenda that could oppose the Republicans.
1.5.1. Con: A coherent policy agenda for the party is the responsibility of the 'professionals' already in charge. New candidates adopt the strategy, not rewrite it.
1.6. Con: [Most](http://maristpoll.marist.edu/wp-content/misc/usapolls/us180108_PBS_NPR/UPDATED_NPR_PBS%20NewsHour_Marist%20Poll_National%20Nature%20of%20the%20Sample%20and%20Tables_January%202018.pdf#page=3) people do not want Oprah as president.
1.6.1. Con: While it is true that a majority of people do not want Oprah to run for President, said study shows that, if it were a head-to-head between Oprah Winfrey and Donald Trump, 50% of people would vote for Winfrey and 39% for Trump, leaving 11% undecided.
1.6.2. Con: Presidents are sometimes elected without winning the majority vote. Both Trump and Bush jr lost the popular vote but were still elected. Therefore, it does not matter if "most" people do not want Oprah to be president.
1.7. Pro: Oprah is qualified for the office of the presidency of the United States.
1.7.1. Pro: Oprah meets the three eligibility requirements for the presidency as outlined in Article II of the US Constitution.[archives.gov](https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/constitution-transcript#toc-article-ii-)
1.7.1.1. Con: Eligibility requirements and qualifications are different kinds of things. So, just because Oprah is eligible does not mean she is qualified.
1.7.2. Pro: Oprah may lack experience in the public sector, but she possesses a wealth of experience growing a business empire.
1.7.2.1. Con: Business and the public sector are different. Experience and success in one does not mean that a person will be successful in the other.

1.7.4.4. Pro: -> See 1.2.1.2.
1.7.4.5. Con: Professional experience in entertainment or business should not be seen as disqualifying. What Oprah may lack in knowledge and experience with public policy, national security, and so forth, may be addressed via cabinet appointments.
1.7.4.6. Con: Oprah meets the three eligibility requirements for the presidency as outlined in Article II of the US Constitution.[archives.gov](https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/constitution-transcript#toc-article-ii-)
1.7.4.6.1. Con: Eligibility requirements and qualifications are different kinds of things. So, just because Oprah is eligible does not mean she is qualified.
