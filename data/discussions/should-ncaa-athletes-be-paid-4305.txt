Discussion Title: Should NCAA Athletes Be Paid?

1. NCAA athletes should be paid
1.1. Con: Few schools in the NCAA can even afford to pay their athletes.
1.1.1. Con: According to sports economists, the [NCAA](https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/03/27/ncaa-pay-student-athletes_n_6940836.html) has more than enough to pay their athletes.
1.1.2. Con: In 2014, the NCAA pulled in approximately [$989 million](https://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/03/11/ncaa-revenue-2014_n_6851286.html). After expenses, their surplus is about $80 million. This is enough to pay their athletes.
1.1.3. Pro: Only [14 programs](https://www.forbes.com/sites/sportsmoney/2011/06/09/the-problems-with-paying-college-athletes/) turn a profit that can afford to pay college athletes, without rely on support from outside institutions.
1.1.4. Con: They can be paid by receiving a set percentage of each school.
1.1.4.1. Con: Each athlete should be paid according to his/her value.  The more "schools" vying for an athlete's services, the higher the pay he/she can negotiate.
1.2. Pro: Compensating athletes in college will limit the corruption involving agents, boosters and others.
1.2.1. Con: Compensation does not deter agents from still using corrupt methods to entice college athletes into professional contracts.
1.3. Con: Players will mismanage their money.
1.3.1. Con: Financial advisory can be provided for these players as part of their compensation.
1.3.2. Con: Many professional athletes do not have anyone to guide their financial decisions, yet this does not prevent them from receiving compensation for their performance.
1.3.3. Pro: As an amateur, they do not have the same financial guidance as a professional.
1.3.3.1. Con: They do not need the same financial guidance as a pro, as they are still in college. The guidance necessary is likely to be different.
1.3.4. Con: The should be only paid equally to the average wage of other student workers on campus.
1.3.4.1. Pro: Student athletes are first students, and therefore should be paid equally to other students participating on campus. \(i.e. dining halls, administrative work, recreation center staff, student research assistants, etc.\).
1.3.4.2. Pro: Being paid student wages ensures that the are compensated for their work without distracting from their studies with large sums of money.
1.3.4.3. Con: -> See 1.1.4.1.
1.4. Con: NCAA pay incentivizes more students to enter sports in college diverting their attention away from academic studies.
1.4.1. Pro: If college athletes are compensated, they'll never have an incentive to go to class.
1.4.1.1. Pro: College athletes may not see the value in continuing their studies if they are receiving compensation from the NCAA, as this amount is likely to be more than they would earn from a profession in their chosen field of study.
1.4.2. Pro: When money enters the collegiate athletic equation, the sport, at its most fundamental level, becomes nothing other than a business.
1.4.2.1. Con: Collegiate athletics is already a business without the compensation.
1.4.2.1.1. Pro: College sports are treated professionally at every level, except when it comes to the players.
1.5. Con: If NCAA athletes are paid, college athletes in other sports will have to be paid and schools cannot afford this.
1.6. Pro: Paying NCAA athletes allows them to afford the necessities of college life.
1.6.1. Pro: Players would be able to actually afford a decent meal at school.
1.7. Pro: Compensating NCAA players can help them better take care of their families.
1.7.1. Pro: Many of these athletes come from urban, low-class families and often leave school early because of the pressure to be the main provider for their family at a young age.
1.7.1.1. Pro: They will be able to send money home to help make up for the income lost with them being away at college.
1.8. Pro: As a compensated player, they will be able to afford proper health care.
1.8.1. Pro: Payment would allow these athletes to receive compensation such as life insurance and medical care for injuries sustained..
1.8.1.1. Pro: This would make sure that athletes were taken care of for a lifetime, after spending a few months or years playing a game for the entertainment of others.
1.8.1.2. Pro: They can receive a stipend commensurate with the sport and relative risk associated with that sport.
1.8.1.2.1. Pro: For example, students in sports that put them at increased risk of a concussion or more severe injuries could be paid more.
1.9. Pro: Other companies are profiting from their hard work, so they should be compensated too.
1.9.1. Pro: Television and academic institutions are profiting off of player performance, therefore, players should receive a portion of that profit.
1.9.1.1. Pro: Academic institutions profit from ticket sales, donations, university payments, media rights, and branding.[2008 College Athletics Revenues](http://www.espn.com/ncaa/revenue)
1.9.1.2. Con: By this same logic, universities and colleges should pay its students since it profits off of its students attendance and performance as well. Profit does not always necessitate a pay out.
1.9.1.3. Pro: The ACC conference generated $403.1M in revenue, $373M that was dispersed to member schools. The SEC dispersed $457.8M.[charlotteobserver.com](http://www.charlotteobserver.com/sports/college/article83238577.html)
1.9.1.4. Con: Tuition is increasing and services for students are also increasing therefore these profits should remain within the institution.
1.9.2. Pro: Video game companies use the likeness of certain college athletes in their games without compensating them. They should be entitled to payment for this.
1.10. Con: Receiving a college degree without student loans is compensation enough.
