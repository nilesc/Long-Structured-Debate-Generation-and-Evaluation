Discussion Title: Should the use of 'chosen' or gender-neutral pronouns be mandatory?

1.1. Con: Mandatory pronouns go against the concept of freedom of speech.
1.1.1. Con: [Freedom of speech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_of_speech) is the capacity of citizens in democratic societies to express their ideas without being censored or sanctioned by the government. How a private citizen wants to be referred to \(including name, status or pronoun\) is not regulated by public institutions.
1.1.1.1. Con: If the law mandates that people have the right to determine how others refer to them, then the government is forcing an implicit set of beliefs on the population. The free flow of ideas will be stifled.
1.1.1.2. Con: How a private citizen wants to be referred to is not regulated by public institutions, therefore, the regulation of pronoun usage is not covered by any restriction, nor any freedom.
1.1.1.2.1. Con: How private citizens interact with each other is indeed regulated. One cannot make false statements about someone else, for example.
1.1.1.2.1.1. Pro: Freedom of speech is not absolute. Between private citizens one can offend another and face repercussions in the form of libel, [defamation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defamation) or calumny.
1.1.1.2.1.2. Con: Actually, one can make false statements about someone else. They are free to make those statements, but not free from the consequences of those statements if they are found to cause harm to another party. That's a matter for the court to decide. 

In any case, the legal basis here is the legislation of harm to another party, not the legislation of language itself.
1.1.1.2.1.2.1. Con: In a similar manner, one can choose to misgender someone repeatedly  and use incorrect pronouns. If laws are in place to regulate that, the person misgendered can sue and go to courts for a judge to decide if an offense has been committed.
