Discussion Title: What is the best way to fight overpopulation?

1. What is the best way to fight overpopulation?
1.1. Improve sexual education.
1.1.1. Pro: [Comprehensive sex education](http://www.advocatesforyouth.org/component/content/article/450-effective-sex-education) has shown to limit the number of unwanted teen pregnancies.
1.1.2. Con: Sex ed has been shown to make teenagers more sexually active.
1.1.2.1. Con: Being sexually active doesn't necessarily lead to more births.
1.1.3. Pro: Comprehensive education about sex and related issues such as STIs and birth control give people the tools to choose how they want to shape their lives.
1.2. Promoting adoption in replacement of birth.
1.2.1. Con: The process of adopting a child is not always feasible or possible due to strict adoption rules and cost.
1.2.1.1. Con: Adoption rules could be loosened, or changed to allow more people such as single parents, same-sex parents, etc, to adopt children.
1.2.1.2. Pro: Same-sex joint adoptions are only legal in [26 countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LGBT_adoption#Legal_status) so far.
1.2.2. Pro: Having one biological child and adopting others gives the parent/s a chance to experience a variety of parenting experiences and gives children up for adoption a home and family.
1.2.3. Pro: This will reduce the birth rate for sure. and also homeless children will get home.
1.2.4. Con: Violates basic principles of individual liberty.
1.2.4.1. Con: Promoting adoption is not the same as imposing adoption. I.e. to suggest that someone should adopt does not preclude their capacity to choose otherwise, and does not violate any principles of individual liberty.
1.2.5. Con: You still have to have birth to have anything to adopt.
1.3. Promote one-child families.
1.3.1. Con: One-child families will provide a big gap between the amount of men and women in a country.
1.3.1.1. Con: There is no evidence that the natural human sex ratio is anything significantly different from 1:1.
1.3.1.2. Pro: Implementing a one-child policy leads to forced or back-door [abortions](https://www.pop.org/chinas-one-child-policy-itself-leads-to-forced-abortions/) due to gender preferences or trying to avoid a punishment for having more than one child.
1.3.2. Pro: We need to halve the population if we are to keep from destroying the earth.
1.3.3. Pro: One-child policy give a right to parent to have their own child as well as to help reducing overpopulation without any catastrophe.
1.3.4. Pro: Better care and focus can be given to single child upbringing.
1.3.5. Con: Promoting one-child families fails to consider those with limited access to contraceptives due to finances, lack of education, or religious restrictions etc.
1.3.6. Con: An only child might grow up with less opportunity to experience sibling love and compassion and will be less likely to be able to learn about sharing at a young age. This might lead to a more selfish and short-sighted population.
1.3.7. Con: Some societies are not enlightened enough to be unbiased in the gender of the child, and will have preferential to certain gender, which will upset the ratio in general.
1.3.8. Con: Decreases liberty
1.4. Propose an maximal age \(like 60\), so that everybody knows when life will end. That way people can plan their life better \(you know your curve of life\) and at the end of your life you actually know you're doing good for others \(making space\).
1.4.1. Con: This infringes the most basic human right to live.
