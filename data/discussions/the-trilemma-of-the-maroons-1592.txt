Discussion Title: The Trilemma of the Maroons

1. The Trilemma of the Maroons.
1.1. Option "One":
Agree and then honor the agreement. Become the slave police.
1.1.1. Pro: We will be the more humane slave police.
1.1.1.1. Pro: We can do it as honorably as it can be done, which is a lot better way to live \(and lead and raise our children\) than being dead or a cheat.
1.1.1.1.1. Pro: If we are the slave police we have greater discretion in implementing the policy, such as choosing between life and death.
1.1.1.1.2. Con: If we are met with resistance or violence during capture, we may have no choice but to respond in a matter that causes harm to them to avoid harm coming to us. Thus, mistreatment may be inevitable depending on the circumstances.
1.1.1.1.2.1. Con: We are less prone to maltreat slaves as we can empathise with them in ways the British cannot.
1.1.1.1.3. Con: From the British perspective this might make us worse at being the slave police as our work would have less of a deterrent effect on slaves who consider escaping.
1.1.1.1.3.1. Con: It is not our obligation to calculate out the benefits of the agreement to the British. If they are unhappy with our interpretation, they can revisit the agreement. But until that time, we need not consider whether our implementation is satisfactory or not.
1.1.1.1.3.1.1. Con: While it is not our obligation to care about the British response, it is in fact in our interest. If they are unhappy and decide to kill us, then that is an undesirable outcome.
1.1.1.1.4. Con: It is highly likely that the returned slaves face severe punishment or even death. How they are treated prior to their return thus makes not much of a difference.
1.1.1.1.5. Pro: -> See 1.1.1.1.2.1.
1.1.1.2. Con: -> See 1.1.1.1.4.
1.1.2. Con: By supporting the British, we would help prop up a regime that is cruel to a large portion of the population.
1.1.2.1. Pro: We are living in the mountains, an area that is difficult to control by the British. Our support is essential for achieving full territorial coverage.
1.1.2.1.1. Pro: As a result, the British would be vastly less effective without us. In this way, we in fact make the regime even worse.
1.1.2.1.2. Con: The plan here is only to work with the British to the minimum extent required. Thus, we are minimizing any future harm also.
