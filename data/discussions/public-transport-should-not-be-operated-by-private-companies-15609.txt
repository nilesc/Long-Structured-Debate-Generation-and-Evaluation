Discussion Title: Public transport should NOT be operated by private companies

1. Public transport should NOT be operated by private companies
1.1. Con: Private companies are in competition with each other and therefore are forced to innovate their service.
1.1.1. Pro: Competition would make transport more affordable by driving prices down.
1.1.2. Pro: Marketplace competition does not necessarily encourage true innovation
1.2. Pro: Privatizing public transport would present a variety of financial obstacles.
1.2.1. Pro: Private transport companies are almost always foreign-owned meaning any profits go overseas rather than benefitting taxpayers and being reinvested in infrastructure: [Revealed: How the world gets rich – from privatising British public services](https://www.independent.co.uk/news/business/news/revealed-how-the-world-gets-rich-from-privatising-british-public-services-9874048.html)
1.2.2. Pro: Contracting out operations can open governments to[lawsuits or other expensive legal problems](http://www.bbc.com/news/uk-19402133)
1.2.3. Pro: If a private operator fails or becomes bankrupt, taxpayers could potentially lose [millions of dollars](https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2017/nov/29/east-coast-rail-franchise-terminated-three-years-early-virgin-trains) in potential lost revenue, or through a bailout.
1.2.4. Pro: Private companies require profits in order to stay sustainable, and many public transit services do not make profits[transportgeography.org](https://transportgeography.org/?page_id=5233)
1.3. Pro: Public transport is an essential public service in many areas of the world.
1.3.1. Pro: Public transport is intended to be easily accessible by all demographics, while privatized transport presents more obstacles to lower-income folk. For example, if someone doesn't have a smartphone then they won't be able to access any app-based ride-sharing services.
1.4. Con: Some public transport services can and do turn a profit, such as systems in Singapore and [Hong Kong.](http://money.cnn.com/2015/03/30/news/hong-kong-mtr-subway-property/index.html)
1.5. Pro: The public has a [negative view](http://www.abc.net.au/news/2015-02-11/lewis-and-woods-voters-still-sceptical-of-privatisation/6083982) of privatisation
1.6. Con: Public ownership and operation is [too expensive and inefficient.](https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/road-and-rail-transport/11810857/A-state-owned-railway-would-be-a-costly-mistake.html)
1.6.1. Pro: One [study](http://www.nber.org/papers/w22088) by the National Bureau of Economic Research found that privatizing bus services could save nearly $6 billion per year in government spending.
1.6.2. Pro: Despite numerous subsidies and regulatory aid, many public transportation systems still seem to exist in a [constant state of financial crisis](https://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2016/10/public-transportation-uber-chariot/505658/).
1.6.3. Con: If UK railways were run in public ownership, [it would save the country £1.2 billion a year](https://weownit.org.uk/public-ownership/railways)
1.7. Pro: Aggressive competition can have a [negative impact](http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/england/manchester/4871214.stm) on services and congestion.
