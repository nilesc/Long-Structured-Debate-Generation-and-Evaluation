Discussion Title: Jack Torrance is actually the hero of the book "The Shining"

1. Jack Torrence is the hero of the book "The Shining"
1.1. Pro: Jack Torrence is much more easily pitied than hated
1.1.1. Pro: Jack has to struggle with the fact that he loved his Father, but his Father was an abusive alcoholic.
1.1.2. Con: One pities Jack because of his own toxic self-pity which is exactly his failing.
1.2. Con: Everything that happens is his fault.
1.2.1. Con: Everything that happens is Danny's fault
1.2.2. Con: Everything that happens is Wendy's fault
1.2.3. Con: Everything that happens is the Overlook's fault.
1.2.4. Pro: He set the timer ahead on George Hatfield, so everything that happened, happened.
1.2.4.1. Pro: "He was just putting George Hatfield out of his misery."
1.2.4.1.1. Con: And he lied about it.
1.3. Con: He was weak and that's why the hotel used him
1.3.1. Con: He doesn't want to do any of the things the hotel gets him to do.
1.3.2. Con: But the fact that he struggled with his weakness is exactly what makes him great.
1.3.2.1. Pro: Just like superman is a terrible hero because he's too great.
1.3.3. Con: Who wouldn't be weak after an abusive childhood?
1.4. Pro: Jack ultimately beats the hotel.
1.5. Con: Jack Torrence is abusive
1.5.1. Con: Being abusive doesn't make him a villain.
1.5.1.1. Pro: He's abusive because parts of him are broken, and he wants to fix those parts, but can't.
1.6. Pro: Jack deeply and truly loves his son.
