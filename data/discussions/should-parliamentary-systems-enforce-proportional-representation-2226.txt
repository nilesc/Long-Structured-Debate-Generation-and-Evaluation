Discussion Title: Should Parliamentary Systems Enforce Proportional Representation?

1. Parliamentary systems should implement proportional representation.
1.1. Pro: Governments should not be able to wield 100% of the power with less than 50% of the vote. Proportional representation puts an end to that.
1.2. Pro: Proportional systems are far harder to gerrymander. If the constituencies return 5 or more representatives then gerrymandering becomes quite impractical.
1.3. Con: The largest party gains a much larger seats than votes and this means that the leading party is more likely to have a stable majority making the government answerable to it \(if the system is parliamentary\) more stable.
1.4. Pro: Proportional representation leads to better political representation.
1.4.1. Pro: The views of the population are accurately represented in parliament.
1.4.1.1. Pro: The results of the election are more representative of the true opinions and stances of the electorate.
1.4.1.2. Pro: The amount of influence a party has over government policy is tied to the popular support they receive.
1.4.1.2.1. Pro: Party seats allow a party to either form government, form the official opposition, or influence policy via threats of votes of confidence, depending on the amount of seats different parties have.
1.4.1.2.1.1. Pro: The number of seats a party receives is proportional to the percentage of popular support they receive during the election.
1.4.2. Pro: Coalitions and deals associated with proportional representation allow a greater amount of the population's views to be reflected in policy.
1.4.2.1. Pro: Coalition and minority governments enact policies that satisfy a majority of the parliaments' representatives and their constituents.
1.4.2.1.1. Pro: Coalitions and minority governments require multiple parties to work together in order to retain the confidence of at least 50%+1 of the house.
1.4.2.1.2. Pro: If other parties feel like the party in power or a party from the coalition government is dissatisfying the electorate, they are able to join forces with other parties and remove them from power.
1.4.2.1.2.1. Pro: This allows the population to have their say in politics in between elections.
1.4.2.1.2.2. Pro: This incentivizes parties in power to keep their promises and act on their mandate.
1.4.2.1.2.3. Pro: This encourages the electorate to express themselves through protests, activism, and lobbying throughout the government's term in office.
1.4.2.2. Pro: Proportional representation often leads to coalition or minority governments.
1.4.3. Con: Coalition and minority governments make it more difficult to figure out who is to blame for the government's actions.
1.4.3.1. Con: Proportional representation leads to more coalition and minority governments.
1.4.3.1.1. Con: It's difficult for a party to obtain over 50% of the popular vote, which means parties must cooperate in order to form government.
1.4.3.2. Con: Coalition and minority governments involve backroom deals where compromises are made without transparency.
1.4.3.3. Pro: Parties are more inclined to hold other parties accountable for their mistakes.

1.4.3.4. Con: Parties may disagree on an issue but are forced to go against promises and toe the line of the government in order to continue to influence it.
1.4.4. Con: Proportional representation makes it more difficult to represent minority and regional interests.
1.4.4.1. Con: Nothing is stopping a party with a regional agenda and platform from existing in a proportional representation system.
1.4.4.2. Con: Most other systems like first past the post have ridings or regions where a member of parliament represents.
