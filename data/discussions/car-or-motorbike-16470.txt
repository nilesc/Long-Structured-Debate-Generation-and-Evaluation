Discussion Title: Car or motorbike?

1. A car is better for long term everyday life.
1.1. Con: A motorbike is better because it can drive between lanes of traffic jam.
1.1.1. Con: Driving between lanes, even in slow traffic, is dangerous.
1.1.2. Pro: In large cities with bad traffic, if one needs a private motorized vehicle, motorbikes can be the only option to avoid traffic jams.
1.1.3. Con: Driving between lanes is illegal in most cases.
1.2. Pro: A car is better because it is safer.
1.2.1. Pro: Four wheels are naturally more stable than two.
1.2.1.1. Pro: Cars are safer than motorbikes on snaking mountain roads.
1.2.1.2. Pro: Cars are more stable in case of strong winds or rain making the road slippery.
1.2.2. Pro: A car have more safety equipment.
1.2.2.1. Pro: You can't fall out of the car thanks to the safety belt.
1.2.2.2. Pro: Airbags are more common and more efficient in cars than in motorbikes.
1.2.3. Con: Safety mostly depends on the driver conduct.
1.2.3.1. Pro: Reducing speed is an effective way to increase safety.
1.2.3.2. Pro: Alertness will spare you many dangers.
1.2.3.3. Con: An equally good car and motorbike driver will [more likely be hurt in a motorbike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motorcycle_safety).
1.2.4. Pro: In the event of an accident, passengers in cars are more likely to survive, but injuries from motorcycle accidents are often fatal.
1.2.4.1. Pro: Unlike car drivers, motorbike riders are not protected by steel and are typically thrown from the bike during a collision.
1.3. Pro: A car is better because it is more comfortable for long drives.
1.3.1. Pro: Cars are typically closed and not exposed to weather conditions.
1.3.1.1. Con: Some people prefer motorbikes because of exposure to wind and the more direct feeling of speed.
1.3.1.2. Pro: Cars can have air conditioning.
1.3.1.2.1. Con: Motorcycles can have air conditioning \([example](https://motorbikewriter.com/air-conditioning-motorcyclists/)\).
1.3.2. Pro: Car seats are more comfortable.
1.3.2.1. Con: Lowrider motorbikes can also offer comfortable seating positions while driving.
1.3.2.2. Con: It all depends on the seat and the seating position for either car or motorcycle. Some cars have uncomfortable seats even for short drives, while some motorbike seats remain comfortable even for long drives.
1.3.2.2.1. Con: The seating position in a car is adjustable for your comfort.
1.4. Con: A motorbike is better because motorbikes allow for more people being transported per square meter of road, than cars.
1.4.1. Con: Not a big difference when you account for a full medium sized car.
1.4.1.1. Con: Unfortunately, cars are often not full.
1.5. Pro: A car can be operated in nearly any weather, while motorbikes are very uncomfortable to operate in rain and very unsafe to operate on snow.
1.6. Con: A motorbike is better for the environment because its production and operation require fewer resources.
1.7. Pro: A car has higher load capacity than a motorbike.
1.7.1. Pro: A car is better because it can carry more luggage, and bigger stuff.
1.7.1.1. Pro: You can buy a laundry machine \(for example\) and carry it by car, while you can't carry it by motorbike.
1.7.1.1.1. Con: You could get these things delivered at home instead of carrying it on your personal vehicle.
1.7.1.1.1.1. Con: Delivery has a cost.
1.7.1.1.1.1.1. Con: In everyday life, we don't often buy large stuff. So the cost of deliveries may still be lower than your savings made by using a motorbike.
1.7.1.2. Pro: A car load can even be increased using a trailer. It can carry large things such as a small boat or a mobile home.
1.7.2. Pro: A car is better because it can carry more passengers.
1.7.2.1. Pro: You can do carpooling.
1.7.2.2. Con: Public transport carries even more passengers. You can use a motorbike for individual drives, and public transport in other cases.
1.7.2.2.1. Con: Public transport is less flexible, with predetermined routes and timetables.
1.7.2.3. Pro: It can carry a whole family.
1.8. Con: A motorbike is better because it is cheaper in many regards \([Example of comparison](https://www.moneysupermarket.com/car-insurance/articles/bike-or-car/) \).
1.8.1. Pro: It uses less fuel.
1.8.2. Pro: Maintenance is cheaper.
1.8.3. Pro: The initial price is cheaper.
1.8.4. Pro: Motorbike insurance is on average cheaper than car insurance.
1.8.5. Con: Driving a motorbike requires expensive equipment such as helmet, gloves, vest etc.
1.8.5.1. Con: The helmet is the highest cost. Yet the cost of the safety gear is more than offset by the savings on fuel and insurance, once you are older than 25.
1.9. Con: A motorbike is better because it can park more easily. You don't have to go in circles looking for a parking space.
1.9.1. Con: In most cities, motorbikes can't just be parked anywhere, but also need to be left at designated parking locations.
1.9.1.1. Con: When most designated parking places are taken, only the smallest ones are left empty, and they are only suitable for motorbikes.
1.9.2. Pro: Motorbikes take up less parking space than cars.
1.9.2.1. Pro: A group of riders may park 2 to 3 bikes per spot when parking is limited.
