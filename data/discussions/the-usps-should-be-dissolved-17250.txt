Discussion Title: The USPS should be dissolved

1. The USPS should be dissolved
1.1. Pro: A dissolved postal service will result in no more spam mail.
1.1.1. Con: Spam mail happens through email as well.
1.2. Con: The internet is not readily accessible to everyone. The mail system is still a necessity for some.
1.2.1. Pro: The USPS delivers to every address, even the bottom of the Grand Canyon.
1.2.2. Pro: Not everyone wants emails, or has a computer.
1.2.3. Con: Years ago, the internet was less accessible for many, but has grown. Its likely it will continue to grow to reach more people.
1.3. Con: Dissolving the USPS would dissolve thousands of jobs.
1.3.1. Pro: The [639,789 employees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service) will loose their jobs.
1.3.1.1. Con: A quarter of whom are already eligible for retirement.
1.3.1.2. Con: Some of the jobs will be replaced by technical jobs in whatever the replacement for the post office would be, or the private sector package couriers
1.3.1.3. Con: The current economy has more jobs available than applicants who can fill them.
1.3.2. Con: A more efficient system has the possibility of employing some of these workers.
1.4. Pro: The US would save $18 billion in tax payer money to reallocate elsewhere.
1.4.1. Pro: [Robert Shapiro](http://fortune.com/2015/03/27/us-postal-service/) estimates that subsidies and legal monopolies cost $18 billion.
1.5. Con: Individuals in rural America will be under served and disconnected from the rest of the population.
1.5.1. Con: Updating the mailing system can help modernize rural areas.
1.5.1.1. Pro: Those in rural areas will be able to connect with those from more developed areas with more ease and speed than available now.
1.6. Pro: The mail system could be replaced by a modern technology, ie email.
1.6.1. Pro: The national government would have a vested interest in the spread of reliable internet across the US.
1.6.1.1. Pro: Internet availability will shrink the income gap.
1.6.1.2. Con: Increased internet regulation.
1.6.2. Con: One antiquated technology could be replaced by another, i.e. email.
1.7. Pro: Having no physical mail saves paper.
1.7.1. Pro: Over [360 million](http://www.theworldcounts.com/stories/Paper-Waste-Facts) tons of paper, and counting, has been produced in 2018, creating tons of paper waste. Removing mail can help reduce this waste substantially.
